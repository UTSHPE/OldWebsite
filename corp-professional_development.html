Professional Development

SHPE National Conference

The SHPE National Conference is the largest Hispanic technical conference in the nation. The conference provides educational, technical, developmental, and networking opportunities for students. The SHPE Conference attracts thousands of students, corporate representatives, and community leaders. The agenda includes: technical and career workshops, a career fair, a graduate institute, corporate tours, the National Academic Olympiad, technical paper competitions, and the Extreme Engineering challenge. The SHPE Conference also offers a Pre-College Symposia which encourages middle school and high school students to explore career opportunities in the STEM fields. UT Austin has a track record of being well represented at the SHPE National Conference. This year, the conference will take place in Cleveland, OH.
(Previous Attendance: 20)

Professional Workshops

Some of the professional workshops SHPE offers are for networking and salary negotiations and offers. The Networking Workshop will teach students how to network in both small groups and a large room of people. Members will know how to properly approach professionals in a comfortable environment with small snacks and drinks. Corporate supporters will be able to share their knowledge and give examples of great networking skills that they use in their company or everyday life. In the salary negotiations and offers workshop, with the help of the Equal Opportunity in Engineering program, we will teach students how to properly negotiate their salary or job offer. Through this workshop, we will allow students to practice negotiating in a professional manner and help them evaluate why their skills are worth negotiating for.
(Expected attendance: 30)

Mock Interview Workshop

This event will allow our supporters to have dedicated one-on-one time with students who would be interested in seeking internships or full-time positions. The interviewer would be responsible for giving valuable feedback about the interview, the candidate’s resume, and general advice about the interview process. The workshop will occur before EXPO and grants you the perfect opportunity to interact with members while you are already in Austin.
(Previous Attendance: 40)

Corporate Tours

SHPE Corporate Tours are informal guided tours of local engineering firms in the Austin area. By taking a look at what engineers do in the workplace, members will gain valuable insight about industry and learn about the possible career paths once they graduate. Corporate supporters will get the opportunity to network with students in a small environment. This is an awesome opportunity for companies to show SHPE members what their company’s culture is like and what they do on a daily basis.
(Previous Attendance: 20)

Lunch & Learn

The SHPE Lunch & Learn is an event meant to provide exclusive access to casual networking and recruitment through out the school year. This event will give students the opportunity to ask question and receive guidance on professional development and recruitment. Corporate supporters will host a presentation to speak about career opportunities, industry insights, or t technical talks about emerging technologies.
(Previous Attendance: 25)

Etiquette Dinner

The SHPE Etiquette Dinner is an annual event co-hosted with the UT Hispanic Business Student Association student chapter. This event will instruct students on how to interact with people during formal or semi-formal lunches or dinners. Corporate supporters will sit with small clusters of students during the dinner and will be able to speak to the students about employments opportunities, current projects in industry, or the roles and responsibilities of engineers.
(Previous Attendance: 45)

Señoritas Professional Dinner

Part of our SHPE Señoritas Program, our chapter will invite the SHPE Austin Professional chapter as well as UT-SHPE alumni to this professional dinner. Female students will network with the professionals in order to learn more about being a woman in industry and to gain engineering positive role models and mentors. This is also a comfortable environment where both students and professionals can discuss common obstacles and how to overcome them, as well as how to lead confidently. This event would be a great opportunity for any corporate supporter who could share their experiences and network with talented women in engineering and STEM fields.
(Previous Attendance: 30)
